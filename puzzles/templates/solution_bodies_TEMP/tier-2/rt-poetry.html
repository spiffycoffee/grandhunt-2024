A 12-line poem with some odd word choices, followed by 12 lines of underscores and numbers 1-6, and

finally a 6-letter answer. Every line seems to contain a weird word. Each word is missing ""AG"".

Thematically - silver is the metal that has been removed.


CHIC[AG]O FOR[AG]E [AG]REED ME[AG]RE MIR[AG]E PASS[AG]ES BAND[AG]ED DAM[AG]E R[AG]ING RAV[AG]ED W[AG]ED SALV[AG]ED


Now for the second part. There are 6 "rather puzzling" clues in the Postscript. You have the find the six

answers from above that fit the clues, in a punning or cryptic wordplay manner, and each clue has an

index; use that to index into the answer


“Who’s da guy throwin’ all da fireballs?” (3) -> DA + MAGE -> M

Stylish, in the past (3) -> CHIC + AGO -> I

“Boy, the Stones sure look older!” (3) -> BAND + AGED -> N

1986 (Russia) – 2001 (South Pacific), so about 15 years (2) -> MIR + AGE -> I

Racing where cold is good (5) -> RACING but replace C with G for RAGING -> N

Right, Ava took US high school equivalency test (5) -> R + AVA + GED -> G


Answer: MINING

Bonus: What does the title "Rt Poetry" mean? rAGtAG Poetry. Just a joke, not part of the solution.
